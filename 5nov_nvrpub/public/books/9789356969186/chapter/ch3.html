INTRODUCTION

A gland is an organ of secretion made of specialized secretory cells derived from the surface epithelium on which it usually opens. Glands can be classified in different ways:

CLASSIFICATION OF GLANDS

  1. Based on the site of secretion:

    • Exocrine: They pour their secretions by the ducts on the surface epithelium, e.g., Salivary glands

    • Endocrine: Are ductless and detached from the epithelium and pour secretion directly into the blood or lymph, e.g., Thyroid, parathyroid, adrenal, etc., and

    • Paracrine: They pour secretion into local extracellular space, e.g., Enteroendocrine cells of GIT.

  2. Based on the number of cells:

    • Unicellular: It consists of one cell only, e.g., Goblet cell

    • Multicellular: It consists of many cells, e.g., All glands other than Goblet cells

  3. Based on the number of ducts and the shape of secretory units:

    • Simple: It has unbranched duct

    • Compound: It has branched duct.21

  1. Based on the mode of secretion:

    • Merocrine or Epicrine gland: Secretion by exocytosis and cells remain intact, e.g., Most of compound gland.

    • Apocrine gland: Apical part of the cell is lost in the process of secretion, e.g., Mammary gland, apocrine sweat gland

    • Holocrine gland: The entire cell bursts and pours contents into the secretion, e.g., Tarsal gland

    • Cytocrine gland: Cells are released as secretion, e.g., Testis spermatozoa.

  2. Based on the nature of secretion:

    • Serous gland: Its secretion is thin and watery, e.g., Parotid gland

    • Mucous gland: Its secretion is thick and viscous, e.g., Sublingual gland

    • Mixed or seromucous gland: Its secretion is both watery and viscous, e.g., Submandibular gland22

Clinical Correlation/Vertical Integration

  • Malignant tumors derived from glandular epithelial tissue are called adenocarcinomas (Gr. adenos, gland, + karkinos, crab + oma, tumor). These are the most common tumors in adults.

  • Adenocarcinoma are common in regions having glandular epithelium such as, colon, breasts, esophagus, lungs, pancreas, and prostate.

  • Carcinomas composed of differentiated cells reflect cell-specific morphological features and behaviors (e.g., the production of cytokeratins, mucins, and hormones). Because these carcinomas usually contain cytokeratins, the detection of these molecules by immunocytochemistry often helps to determine the diagnosis and treatment of these tumors.
