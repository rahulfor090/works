7Preface to the Eighth Edition

Standing at the crossroads of a rapidly changing environment and a world where technology revolutionizes each aspect of our life, our eyes continue to witness big and small events every day that accumulate and change the course of human history. When the seventh edition of Essentials of Ophthalmology was published in 2019, we did not know that we were at the brink of a life-alerting pandemic that would go on for years. Or that our systems of healthcare and research would continue to be in flux for the foreseeable future.

In the case of ophthalmology too, there has been a considerable evolution in the way that diseases are now diagnosed and managed, new surgical techniques have been adopted, and changes have been made to hospital policies and medical management systems. In bringing out this new edition, I have reviewed and reflected on all such changes that have taken place since 2019. In this eighth edition, readers will find: updated chapters on new investigation processes, surgical procedures, disease control and management. The chapter on community ophthalmology, ‘Blindness and Prevention of Blindness’ has been thoroughly changed as per National Programme for Control of Blindness and Visual Impairment (NPCB & VI), Government of India. 8Preface to the First Edition

Most medical colleges do not have a required undergraduate teaching course in ophthalmology. Consequently, the students are often sadly deficient in this subject, unless they are specially interested. In addition, an acute shortage of time, prevents the students from reading large textbooks on this subject. Many students complain that most of the available books are either too brief or too extensive. It is with the hope of bridging this gap that I have written this book.

This book focusses on the information in a concise, step-wise, outline format which is essential for getting a true picture of the subject. The arrangement of the subject matter is to-the-point, and up-to-date with plenty of line diagrams which are easily reproducible during examination. Though this book is essentially meant for the undergraduate students, I hope, it will be very useful to the postgraduate students as well as practising ophthalmologists, for a quick revision of the subject.

In conclusion, I shall be happy if this book proves itself to be a valuable guide both for the undergraduate and postgraduate students, especially during their examination. Any critical comment and suggestion from the students and teachers, regarding this effort, will be appreciated.

Samar K Basak
